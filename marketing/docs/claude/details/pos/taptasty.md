## Target Market

- **Business types**: Restaurants, pizzerias, QSR, cafés, bars, food delivery businesses
- **Size**: SMB and independent restaurants; chain/franchise support via multi-location management features
- **Geographic focus**: Romania (primary home market); FiscalNet integration is specific to Romanian fiscal compliance, making the product currently most natural for Romanian operators

## Hardware Model

- **BYOD + POS integration model** — TapTasty does not sell hardware; it integrates with existing POS systems
- Kiosk: BYOD tablet on stand; TapTasty provides the software and the kiosk interface
- FiscalNet ensures compatibility with Romanian bank POS terminals and fiscal printers
- Mobile app: customer's own iOS/Android devices
- Driver app: delivery driver's own iOS/Android device

## Strengths vs. Sciometa POS

| Strength | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Deep Romanian market integration | FiscalNet + 12+ local POS integrations = natural fit for Romanian restaurants without requiring POS replacement |
| Modular, additive approach | Restaurants add TapTasty on top of existing POS — low switching cost and risk |
| Branded white-label mobile app | Full React Native branded app (not a generic marketplace app) — strong customer loyalty vehicle |
| NLP ordering assistant | Early-mover AI feature for restaurant ordering — differentiating in the Romanian/Eastern European market |
| Loyalty program breadth | Points earned at kiosk, QR, and in-store via OCR receipt scanning — covers all ordering channels |
| Strong delivery integration | Own driver app + delivery aggregator integration + map view — complete delivery stack |

## Weaknesses vs. Sciometa POS

| Weakness | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Very small company | ~11 employees; limited capacity for enterprise sales, support, or product development |
| No standalone POS | TapTasty does not replace POS — requires an existing POS system; not a full POS alternative |
| Romanian-market dependency | FiscalNet and local POS integrations anchor the product to Romania; international expansion is nascent |
| Opaque pricing | No public pricing; contact-required quoting process slows sales and comparison shopping |
| No shift management | No employee scheduling, payroll, or attendance features |
| No reservations | Table reservation functionality absent |
| Limited inventory | No standalone inventory management — dependent on integrated POS system |
| No disclosed funding | Pre-accelerator stage only; scaling capital not secured; growth limited by resources |
