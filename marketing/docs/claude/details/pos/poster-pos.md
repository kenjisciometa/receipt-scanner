## Pricing

### Subscription Plans (billed annually)

| Plan | Monthly cost | Key inclusions |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Mini | $26/month | Up to 100 products/recipes; POS app (iOS/Android/Windows/Mac); inventory; loyalty; QR menu; AI assistant; P&L reports; 24/7 chat support |
| Business | $44/month (most popular) | Up to 300 products/recipes; all Mini features + dish modifiers, promotions, delivery management, payroll calculation, mobile waiter app, KDS; 24/7 phone support |
| Pro | $62/month | Up to 1,500 products/recipes; all Business features + table reservations, backdated menu edits, butchery management, advanced access control |
| Custom (Enterprise) | Contact sales | 1,500+ dishes or restaurant chains |

*Note: Capterra lists slightly higher prices ($29/$49/$69) — likely reflects USD vs. regional pricing or recent adjustments.*

## Strengths vs. Sciometa POS

| Strength | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Lowest price point in class | Mini at $26/month is among the cheapest full-featured cloud POS available |
| Postie AI is genuinely differentiated | Natural language analytics, invoice OCR, bank statement import, sales forecasting in one add-on — practical AI vs. marketing AI |
| Excellent user ratings | 4.9/5 on Capterra (70 reviews); 99% positive sentiment — exceptional for a POS product |
| 15-day Pro trial — no credit card | Low friction trial removes a major acquisition barrier |
| Cross-platform BYOD | Works on iOS, Android, Windows, Mac — widest device compatibility |
| Strong inventory at low cost | Recipe-level ingredient tracking included from $26/month |
| 110-country reach | True global product, not region-locked |

## Weaknesses vs. Sciometa POS

| Weakness | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| No native kiosk product | Self-ordering limited to QR; no kiosk hardware or software module |
| Add-on costs accumulate | Base price is low but Postie AI + QR + Site + waiter app + extra registers can push monthly cost to $100+ |
| Per-product caps feel restrictive | 100/300/1,500 product limits per tier can frustrate growing businesses |
| Limited enterprise features | Multi-concept chain management is basic (Poster Connect); not suited for 50+ location groups |
| Accounting integration narrow | Only Xero and Cloudbeds natively; others require API work |
| Security/permissions gaps | Users report limited role-based access control between staff members |
| Ukrainian HQ risk | Geopolitical uncertainty around Ukraine-based operations is a concern for some buyers |
